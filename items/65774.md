## Summary

Current main still does not satisfy the safety behavior requested here. Startup catch-up can replay overdue or missed cron slots immediately, cron remove/disable does not abort an already-started run, gateway shutdown only stops future cron timer wakes, and there is no cron/gateway emergency stop surface for active outbound delivery. The related maintenance-window work remains only a candidate in the provided context, not a shipped fix.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep this open and fix it in cron/gateway core. Add schedule-window-aware startup catch-up or explicit deferred replay semantics, keep a service-level registry of active cron AbortControllers, make cron.remove/disable and gateway shutdown abort matching active executions, add an explicit emergency stop or outbound pause surface, and add regression coverage for an America/Guatemala out-of-hours reconnect plus deletion/abort of an active WhatsApp-style delivery. If #55160 becomes the canonical design, review and land it or a narrower version first; close this only after the safety behavior ships and is verified.

## Risks / Open Questions

- Delayed cron messages may still be sent outside a user's intended contact window after gateway reconnect or restart.
- Deleting or disabling a cron job, or stopping the gateway, may not cancel a WhatsApp-style isolated delivery that has already started.
- A fix needs careful task/run/session accounting so aborted deliveries do not produce misleading success, failure, or lost-run state.
