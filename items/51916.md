## Summary

Keep open. Current `main` partially addresses the report by requesting Telegram business update types, but it does not implement the requested Telegram Business message/connection handling in the Telegram channel plugin.

## Evidence

- **Business update types are now requested:** `resolveTelegramAllowedUpdates()` includes `business_connection`, `business_message`, `edited_business_message`, and `deleted_business_messages`, and both polling and webhook setup pass that list to Telegram.
  - file: [extensions/telegram/src/allowed-updates.ts:8](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/blob/aa3a5f9ff7907c2a41d6259d24b529bda41f293a/extensions/telegram/src/allowed-updates.ts#L8)
  - command: `nl -ba extensions/telegram/src/allowed-updates.ts | sed -n '1,120p'; nl -ba extensions/telegram/src/monitor.ts | sed -n '24,42p'; nl -ba extensions/telegram/src/webhook.ts | sed -n '415,435p'`
  - sha: [aa3a5f9ff790](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/commit/aa3a5f9ff7907c2a41d6259d24b529bda41f293a)
- **No business handlers in the Telegram runtime:** The inbound handler wiring on current `main` registers `bot.on("message")` and `bot.on("channel_post")`, but there is no corresponding `bot.on("business_message")`, `bot.on("business_connection")`, `bot.on("edited_business_message")`, or `bot.on("deleted_business_messages")` handler.
  - file: [extensions/telegram/src/bot-handlers.runtime.ts:1939](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/blob/aa3a5f9ff7907c2a41d6259d24b529bda41f293a/extensions/telegram/src/bot-handlers.runtime.ts#L1939)

- History is shallow here, so I could not attribute when the `allowed_updates` change landed, only that the current `main` still lacks the broader feature requested.
