## Summary

Keep open. Current `main` does not apply the PR's advertised persistence-time image stripping, and the PR's reviewed revision turned into a no-op hook after valid replay/recovery concerns were raised. That leaves an unresolved bug/design question rather than a safe maintainer-cleanup close.

## Risks / Open Questions

- The underlying disk-bloat concern may still be real, because current `main` prunes images from replay context but does not clearly rewrite existing session JSONL entries.
- The PR as last discussed is not a safe cleanup close: it contains useful analysis about the replay-safety constraint, but not a completed landed fix.
- A correct follow-up likely needs a transcript-rewrite or maintenance-path solution that preserves pending image payloads until they are safely consumable.
