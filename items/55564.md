## Summary

Current `main` still intentionally replays pending outbound delivery-queue entries on gateway startup, and the code/tests do not show any stale-age TTL or restart-drop behavior for those queued messages. The reported flood after restart remains plausible on current `main`, so this item should stay open.

## Evidence

- **Write-ahead outbound queue persists messages before send:** Outbound delivery still enqueues a disk-backed delivery-queue entry before sending, so interrupted sends survive restarts and remain eligible for later replay.
  - file: [src/infra/outbound/deliver.ts:762](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/blob/95b7a85f0607784d8e33b4ce7e42617174196490/src/infra/outbound/deliver.ts#L762)
  - command: `nl -ba src/infra/outbound/deliver.ts | sed -n '730,830p'`
  - sha: [95b7a85f0607](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/commit/95b7a85f0607784d8e33b4ce7e42617174196490)
- **Startup recovery retries all pending queued deliveries:** `recoverPendingDeliveries()` loads all pending entries, sorts by `enqueuedAt`, and retries them on startup; there is no age cutoff or stale-message discard path in this recovery loop.
  - file: [src/infra/outbound/delivery-queue-recovery.ts:343](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/blob/95b7a85f0607784d8e33b4ce7e42617174196490/src/infra/outbound/delivery-queue-recovery.ts#L343)
