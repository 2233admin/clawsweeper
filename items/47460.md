## Summary

Current `main` already contains Telegram restart/startup protections that specifically prevent queued or in-flight updates from being skipped during polling restarts, plus voice-note preflight transcription on inbound Telegram audio. That covers the reported "file saved but never injected" startup-race class well enough to close as implemented.

## Evidence

## Risks / Open Questions

- The original report was on `2026.3.8`; if a silent drop still occurs on newer builds, it would likely be a different post-download dispatch failure than the startup/update-offset race described here.

## Close Comment

Closing after Codex review: current `main` already has the Telegram startup/restart protections that address this silent-drop class. In `extensions/telegram/src/bot-core.ts:287`, OpenClaw persists only a safe update watermark bounded by pending/failed `update_id`s so restart cannot skip an in-flight lower update; in `extensions/telegram/src/polling-session.ts:228`, polling startup confirms the persisted offset with Telegram before the runner starts; and `extensions/telegram/src/bot.create-telegram-bot.test.ts:1149` plus `extensions/telegram/src/monitor.test.ts:766` cover those cases directly. Current main also preflight-transcribes inbound DM voice notes in `extensions/telegram/src/bot-message-context.body.ts:200`, with regression coverage in `extensions/telegram/src/bot-message-context.body.test.ts:149`. These protections are already documented as shipped in `CHANGELOG.md:1240`, `CHANGELOG.md:3214`, and `CHANGELOG.md:4295`, and are present in release `v2026.4.22` (`00bd2cf7a376f1fba26291c6c4766f1f15cbdfa5`). If you still hit a drop on a newer build, please open a fresh issue with exact version, logs around the update id, and whether the failure happened before or after message dispatch began.
