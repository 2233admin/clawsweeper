## Summary

Current main still leaves the reported gateway auth-none internal RPC failure unresolved. The source still treats `auth.mode="none"` as an auth-layer success only; probe clients can intentionally connect without device identity, `method: "none"` is not counted as shared auth, and backend/internal callers are only exempted from self-pairing when they use token/password shared auth or a device token. The related #69066 RFC is relevant but remains open design work, not a shipped fix.

## What This Changes

The issue reports that `gateway.auth.mode="none"` causes Gateway-owned probe and follow-up RPC clients to fail with `1008: device identity required`, which breaks exec approval followups and can make streamed replies disappear after reconnect.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep this issue open and fix it through a first-class internal-service identity path, or a very narrow loopback-only internal caller exception, for Gateway-owned probe/followup/backend RPC clients under `gateway.auth.mode="none"`. Preserve fail-closed behavior for external users, browsers, node-role clients, and non-internal operator clients, and coordinate the durable design with #69066.
