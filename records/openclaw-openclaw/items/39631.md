## Summary

Keep #39631 open. Current main at 8ba82534e6c98410e19b226f3534b285fd75d278 still forces mutating tool failures such as failed edit/write into user-facing `⚠️ ... failed` payloads, and the Telegram dispatcher still delivers final `isError` payloads rather than suppressing or normalizing them. The supplied related PR context shows #39636 and #39717 were closed unmerged, while broader #39406 remains open and does not by itself resolve this Telegram-specific report.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep the issue open until OpenClaw deliberately changes user-facing delivery for failed mutating tools. The likely fix belongs around `resolveToolErrorWarningPolicy` and `buildEmbeddedRunPayloads`, with Telegram dispatch regression coverage: suppress or normalize failed `edit()`/mutating-tool warnings when a clean assistant reply exists, keep full details in logs/debug/verbose surfaces, preserve a safe fallback when no assistant reply exists, and coordinate the broader config policy with #39406.
