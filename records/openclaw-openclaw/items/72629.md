## Summary

Keep open. This is a current design issue with concrete cost observations and community input; current main has sub-agent isolation, bounded history recall, context-engine, compaction, usage, and concurrency primitives, but no settled product boundary or built-in policy for tiered conversational context delivery, shared decision summaries, saliency routing, or pessimistic multi-agent budget reservation.

## What This Changes

The issue asks OpenClaw maintainers and the community to decide how to preserve emergent conversational multi-agent coordination while reducing token-cost growth at larger agent and round counts.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep the design thread open until maintainers choose the boundary: a generic coordination/cost-governance primitive, context-engine/plugin hooks for tiered delivery and budget reservation, or explicit docs that these policies belong in scenario-specific orchestrators.
